➡️cold coffee

Cold coffee is a refreshing, caffeinated beverage made by blending or pouring chilled
milk, coffee, and sugar over ice. It is highly customizable and comes in many forms
ranging from simple iced black coffee to thick, creamy, milkshake-like drinks often topped with ice cream or whipped cream.

➡️milk coffee

Depending on the ratio of coffee to milk and how the milk is frothed, you will find several popular varieties at cafes worldwide
(1):Flat White: A double espresso mixed with heated milk and a very thin, velvety microfoam layer.
(2)Latte (Caffé Latte): One or two shots of espresso with a larger volume of steamed
milk and a light, thin layer of foam on top.Cappuccino: Traditionally 1/3 espresso, 1/3 steamed milk, and 1/3 thick, airy milk foam.Cortado / Macchiato: Espresso 'stained' or balanced with a small amount of warm milk.Mocha: Espresso combined with steamed milk and chocolate syrup, usually topped with whipped cream. Nescafe

➡️black coffee

Black coffee is a simple, calorie-free beverage made purely from roasted coffee beans and water, without any milk, cream, or added sugar. It is highly prized for its bold, intense flavor and ability to provide a quick, natural energy boost.
🌟 Key Health BenefitsBecause it lacks additives, black coffee retains the bean's natural nutrients and antioxidants: Weight Management: It is virtually calorie-free and boosts metabolism, which can aid in fat burning.Enhanced
Focus: The natural caffeine blocks adenosine (a neurotransmitter that causes fatigue), improving mental alertness and physical endurance
Rich in Antioxidants: It is a major source of polyphenols, which help fight cellular damage and reduce inflammation.Disease Prevention: Moderate, regular consumption has been linked to a reduced risk of type 2 diabetes, liver disease, and neurodegenerative conditions.
